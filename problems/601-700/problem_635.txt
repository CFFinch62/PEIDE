Problem 635: Subset Sums

Let $A_q(n)$ be the number of subsets, $B$, of the set $\{1, 2, ..., q \cdot n\}$ that satisfy two conditions: 1) $B$ has exactly $n$ elements; 2) the sum of the elements of $B$ is divisible by $n$. E.g. $A_2(5)=52$ and $A_3(5)=603$. Let $S_q(L)$ be $\sum A_q(p)$ where the sum is taken over all primes $p \le L$. E.g. $S_2(10)=554$, $S_2(100)$ mod $1\,000\,000\,009=100433628$ and $S_3(100)$ mod $1\,000\,000\,009=855618282$. Find $S_2(10^8)+S_3(10^8)$. Give your answer modulo $1\,000\,000\,009$.
